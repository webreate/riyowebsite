INDIA ON A JOURNEY

India! A country on a journey to become the world leader in Manufacturing and export. Currently, at 3 trillion US dollars, India’s GDP is estimated to become 3rd largest in the world by the end of this decade. For a country to progress and uplift itself as a society, its Logistics infrastructure has to be developed in such a way that its unwanted expense of both time and wealth are minimised hence optimising its output. India’s logistics infrastructure comprises of three major ways of transport

  1. The Roadways
  2. The Waterways
  3. The Airways

all three have come a long way in recent years, but there is still a long way to go to reach the level of developed countries. the future of India's logistics infrastructure looks promising, with the government and private sector working together to improve the sector's performance.

A major challenge facing India's logistics infrastructure is a lack of standardisatio . The country's logistics sector is highly fragmented, with many small and medium-sized enterprises operating in the space. This lack of standardisation makes it difficult to coordinate logistics activities and results in inefficiencies in the supply chain. Another challenge to the country’s logistic industry is the lack of coordination between different players in the supply chain. The country's logistics sector is highly fragmented, and there is a lack of communication and cooperation between differentactors. This makes it difficult to manage logistics activities and results in inefficiencies in the supply chain.

ROADWAYS OF INDIA.

Roadways play a crucial role in a country's development as they are the backbone of a country's transportation system. They provide the means for people and goods to move around, which is essential for economic growth and development .roadways are an essential component of a country's infrastructure and play a vital role in its development. A well-developed road network can help to improve connectivity, economic growth, social development, tourism and regional development The Indian government's plans for reforming the logistics industry aim to create a more efficient and cost-effective logistics ecosystem in the country. The focus is on improving infrastructure, reducing costs, and making logistics more transparent and efficient. These initiatives will help to attract investment, create jobs, and ultimately drive economic growth.
